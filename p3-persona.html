Persona & Jobs To Be Done

Who we're designing for and what they actually need to accomplish

⚡ AI-assisted — persona constructed from research synthesis, validated against B2B sales manager role patterns
MK
Marcin Kowalski, 41
Regional Sales Manager — Central Europe · DataFlow client
"I don't need more data. I need someone to tell me what the hell is actually happening."

Background

  • →15 years in B2B sales, 4 as manager
  • →Team of 12 reps across 3 countries
  • →Promoted for being the best closer, not a data analyst
  • →Reports to VP of Sales every Monday

KPIs he's measured on

  • →Quarterly revenue vs. quota
  • →Pipeline coverage ratio (3x = healthy)
  • →Win rate & average deal size
  • →Forecast accuracy
  • →Team activity metrics

Frustrations

  • →"11 dashboards, none tell me what to do"
  • →Notices cold deals too late to intervene
  • →Rebuilds same report manually every Monday
  • →Reps treat CRM updates as punishment
  • →"I'm managing data, not people"

Goals

  • →Know which deals will close this quarter
  • →Spot problems before they become crises
  • →Answer VP questions in under 2 minutes
  • →Coach reps effectively, not just report on them
  • →Spend less time in the CRM

5 Core Jobs To Be Done
J1
Morning Triage Daily ritual
"When I start my day, I want to immediately know what changed overnight and what needs my attention — so I don't waste the first hour figuring out where I am."
J2
Early Warning Risk mgmt
"When a deal or region starts going wrong, I want to be alerted before it becomes a crisis — so I can intervene while there's still time."
J3
Explainability On Demand Reporting up
"When my VP asks why numbers dropped, I want to answer in 2 minutes with confidence — so I look like I have control of my region."
J4
Decision Support Strategic
"When I'm deciding where to focus my team's energy, I want a recommendation I can trust — so I stop going on instinct alone."
J5
Forecast Confidence Forecasting
"When I submit my quarterly forecast, I want to know which deals I can actually count on — so I stop guessing and commit to realistic numbers."
Success Scenario

Marcin opens DataFlow at 8am. In 90 seconds he knows: what's at risk, what's trending up, what one thing he should do first. He closes the app and talks to his team. He opens it again at 3pm to check if anything changed. He never builds a report — the answer is already there.
