Next Steps: Success Metrics

Measuring outcomes, not just engagement

This project addresses two distinct Jobs to Be Done. Success is defined not by engagement alone, but by measurable improvement in outcomes for the Sales Manager and their team.

Measurement Philosophy

Behavioral metrics serve as diagnostic signals — they tell us whether the feature is being used and how. Outcome metrics are the north star — they tell us whether the feature is actually working. We design for outcomes; we use behavioral data to debug when outcomes don't arrive.

J1 — Morning Triage: "I know what needs my attention today"

Metric Layer How to measure Target
Time-to-first-action Behavioral Time from login to first action (call, email, deal update) logged in CRM 40% reduction vs. baseline
Briefing open rate Behavioral % of working days the AI Briefing screen is opened >80% within first 30 days
Alert click-through rate Behavioral % of AI alerts that result in a logged action >60% (low rate = alerts are not relevant)
Deals without follow-up Outcome % of active deals with no contact logged in >7 days 30% reduction vs. previous quarter

J2 — Early Warning: "I know about the problem before it becomes a crisis"

Metric Layer How to measure Target
Alert-to-action time Behavioral Time between alert display and Marcin's first response <24h for high-priority alerts
False positive rate Behavioral % of alerts dismissed by the user as irrelevant <20% (higher rate signals poor AI signal quality)
Problem detection lag Outcome How many days earlier problems are identified vs. previous quarter −5 days on average
Quota attainment Outcome % of monthly/quarterly target achieved by the team +10% after 2 full quarters

A note on measurement timing

Behavioral metrics are available immediately after launch and can be tracked from day one using product analytics.

Outcome metrics, however, require at least one full sales quarter to produce meaningful data. Quota attainment, deal loss rates, and problem detection lag are influenced by many variables beyond the product — measuring them too early risks drawing false conclusions.

Recommended measurement schedule
  • — Week 1–4 post-launch: behavioral metrics only — monitor adoption, alert engagement, and early drop-off signals
  • — After Quarter 1: first outcome metric review — evaluate detection lag and deal follow-up rate
  • — After Quarter 2: full success evaluation — quota attainment and longitudinal behavioral trends
